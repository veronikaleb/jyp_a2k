Republic Records

← Main page

Republic Records – an American record label owned by Universal Music Group. Located in New York. It was founded by Avery and Monte Lipman as an independent label in 1995 and was acquired by UMG in 2000. Originally part of the Universal Motown Republic Group, Republic was renamed after a reorganization in 2006 on Universal Republic Records, and was reverted to its original name in 2012.

  • Owner: Universal Music Group
  • Founded: 1995
  • Founders: Monte Lipman, Avery Lipman
  • Distributor: Universal Music Distribution, Island Records, Universal Music Enterprises
  • Genres: various
  • Country: USA
  • Location: New York City, New York, USA
  • Website: www.republicrecords.com

History

According to Avery Lipman, he and his brother Monte conceived the idea of creating Republic Records in their apartment in New York. Lippman claims that both of them used to work at record companies in their spare time. They started releasing records as a hobby, the first being Dingleberry Haze by the Bloodhound Gang. Republic Records was established in 1995 as a subsidiary of Geffen Records, but soon after its establishment became a distributor the recently established company Universal Records.

In January 2000, it was announced that Universal Music Group had acquired the Lipman brothers' Republic Records as a subsidiary, which is fully owned. Monte Lipman was named president of Universal Records, and Avery Lipman became president of Republic.

In October 2012, Universal Republic Records reverted to the name Republic Records. Shortly before the label returned to under the name Republic, he was signed by Canadian musician The Weeknd through his label XO. In addition to artists' albums, label released soundtracks with Fox Broadcasting Company (The Star), NBC (The Voice) and former parent company UMG Universal Pictures, along with many other film and TV series partnerships. In 2015, Republic Records songs took six of the top 10 spots on the Mediabase Top 40 chart, breaking the record set in 2013. Also in 2015, the label signed a contract with American rapper and singer Post Malone.

In November 2017, the company was named "Hitmaker Label of the Year" by Variety. In 2018, Taylor Swift signed a contract with Republic Records and released music through Republic's imprint, Big Machine Records, for most of her career. In December of the same In 2010, Republic in cooperation with Sony Pictures released the soundtrack for the movie "Spider-Man: Across the Universe". The main single of the soundtrack Post Malone and Swae Lee's "Sunflower" became the number one song on the Billboard Hot 100, breaking the record for most weeks a top ten stay on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at 45. Early 2019 also saw the signing of the reunited Jonas Brothers.

Also in 2019, Republic Records was named Label of the Year by Billboard and Variety. It became the label of the year according to the version Billboard for 4 of the last 5 years and by Variety for the last three years. Republic was also named as a label of the year on the Billboard Hot 100 for the sixth time in a row. Five Republic albums (Thank U, Ariana Grande's Next, Taylor Swift's Lover, Beerbongs & Bentleys Post Malone, Drake's Scorpion and Post Malone's Hollywood's Bleeding) also entered the Billboard 200 year-end top ten.

In 2020, Republic Records signed a deal with JYP Entertainment to release South Korean group Twice, becoming the first American label and distributor of the group. Twice will be the first artist from JYP Entertainment to receive support under this deal. Signature Twice symbolizes the rise of K-pop in the US.On April 8, 2020, cooperation with the South Korean group (G)I-dle was announced as part of release of their third mini-album, I Trust, which marked the band's official US debut.

Republic Records was the first label to sign a deal with DistroKid, giving it access to the data companies to find new performers. The project started in 2021 and allowed DistroKid to be rewarded for finding any new one an artist signed to the label. As of January 2021, the label has released five of the top seven albums on the Billboard 200 chart, including three top spots with Morgan Wallen's Dangerous: The Double Album, Taylor Swift's Evermore and Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon Pop Smoke. The Weeknd's After Hours and Ariana Grande's Positions came in sixth and seventh respectively. Republic also had six that month of the top ten albums on the Rolling Stone Albums Chart and nine of the top 20 songs on the Rolling Stone Songs Chart. In March 2021 Taylor Swift became the label's first female artist to win a Grammy Award for Album of the Year for Folklore. November 24, 2021 Jim Roppo and Wendy Goldstein have been named co-presidents of Republic Records.

In July 2022, Universal Music Group officially launched and expanded the Republic Records label network in international markets. Label started its operations in the Philippines through its local subsidiary UMG Philippines.

In 2023, Republic Records had the longest streak for a label atop the Billboard 200 in 25 years, including Morgan Wallen's One Thing at a Time, Taylor Swift's Midnights, and Stray Kids' 5-STAR. As of midyear 2023, Republic Records is leading U.S. record label market share with "a relatively surprising level of domination.
