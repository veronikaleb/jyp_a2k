JYP Entertainment

← Main page

JYP Entertainment - a South Korean record label and talent agency founded in May 1997 in Seoul. Singer and music producer Park Chinen is the founder and owner. This is one of the biggest of entertainment companies in the Republic of Korea, which operates as a record label, talent agency, music production company, event organization, concert production company and music publishing company. In addition, the company operates various subsidiaries and divisions around the world.

  • Owner: Park Jin-young
  • Founded: 25.04.1997
  • Key people: Park Jin-young, Jung Wook
  • Genres: K-pop, R&B, EDM, Trot
  • Country: South Korea
  • Location: Gangdong, Seoul, South Korea
  • Website: www.jype.com

JYP history

JYP Entertainment was founded in 1997 by Park Jin-young and at that time had a different name - Tae-Hong Planning Corporation. which eventually became JYP Entertainment in 2001. In 1997, the company signed a contract with its first artist, singer Pearl. In 1999, SidusHQ introduced singer-songwriter and JYP Entertainment founder Park Chin-yeon as their producer and mentor.

In 2006, the MTV show Wonder Girls was launched and it was announced that the Wonder Girls would be JYP's first girl group. They made their debut in February 2007 with the single "Irony", and already in July, Hyena left the team at the request of her parents, and she was replaced by Yubin. In the next ones for several years, the group underwent multiple line-up changes, and the quartet of Yein, Yubin, Sonmi, and Lim became the finale. In February 2017 year, after 10 years of existence, Wonder Girls disbanded. In 2008, the debut of two boy bands took place at once - 2AM and 2PM. They are together unofficially formed the band One Day. In 2009, Jay Park left JYP Entertainment after a scandal over remarks about Korea, and in 2015, three members of 2AM also left the agency at once. Jo Kwon left the company in September 2017.

During Wonder Girls' active promotion period in the US after the success of the single "Nobody", JYP Entertainment will debut another girl group - miss A, which was originally called the "Chinese Wonder Girls". Their debut single "Bad Girl Good Girl" was a smash hit, and the next few followed years, they also released a number of equally successful albums. In May 2016, after the expiration of the contract, the team leaves Ja, after another year, Lee Min-eun leaves the agency. In December 2017, the band was officially disbanded. In 2012, two projects will debut at once: JJ Project and duet 15&. January 2014 saw the debut of GOT7, the first boy band since 2PM's debut. In October 2015, the girl group TWICE will debut, and a month earlier - the instrumental boy band Day6.

Stray Kids officially debuted on March 25, 2018. On January 21, 2019, JYP Entertainment announced that they will be debuting a new girl group under the name ITZY, the band's official YouTube account was launched on the same day, and the label's official channel shared a prologue film that reveals five members of the group. On February 12, the band released their debut single album, It'z Different, featuring the single "Dalla Dalla". On March 11, 2019, JYP Entertainment entered into a new partnership with Sony Music Entertainment, The Orchard. JYPE will distribute digital and physical releases in key markets in the US, Europe and beyond through The Orchard to "expand the label's global presence". On July 24th, JYP Entertainment announced the closure of their acting division and some of their actors will be transferring to a new company. A new company was created by JYP Entertainment Vice President Pio Chen Rok under the name NPIO Entertainment. In a statement, JYP Entertainment announced that their popular actors such as Jang Hee Ryun, Park Si In, and Ryu Won will become free agents. On January 6, 2020, Shinhan Card announced that they have collaborated with JYP to release the "JYP Fan EDM Control Card" (JYP Control Cards). Certain percentages of the amount used when making payments at local and international JYP branches using check cards, are donated to Make-A-Wish Korea in accordance with the EDM campaign. Check cards are available in four types: JYP, Got7, Day6 and Twice. On August 4, 2020, JYP announced its collaboration with SM to create Beyond LIVE Corporation (BLC), a virtual concert joint venture. BLC was created to develop the Beyond Live online concert series with the aim of further transforming the platform into an international online concert brand. Later in an interview with Forbes magazine published on August 31, Park Chinen shared that a girl group survival show is in talks in America, similar to the Sixteen and Nizi Project. On December 1, 2020, the Japanese girl group NiziU debuted, the main part of the promotion of which will take place in Japan, but there will also be activities on the territory of Korea. On November 17, 2020, it was reported that JYP Entertainment invested 5 billion kroner from Naver Z, the developer of Zepeto's online avatar app.

Also in 2019, Republic Records was named Label of the Year by Billboard and Variety. It became the label of the year according to the version Billboard for 4 of the last 5 years and by Variety for the last three years. Republic was also named as a label of the year on the Billboard Hot 100 for the sixth time in a row. Five Republic albums (Thank U, Ariana Grande's Next, Taylor Swift's Lover, Beerbongs & Bentleys Post Malone, Drake's Scorpion and Post Malone's Hollywood's Bleeding) also reached the top ten of the year-end Billboard 200 chart.

On January 11, 2021, it was announced that all members of GOT7 were leaving the agency, but the group was not disbanded. On January 19, GOT7 left the agency, retaining the band name and all intellectual property upon departure. On April 26, it was announced that JYP and P-Nation would be collaborating together to to form a new boy band through the survival show Loud, which premiered on June 5 on SBS. On July 12, JYP announced the second season of the Nizi Project for an upcoming Japanese boy band in 2023. On November 1, the company announced the second men's instrumental group Xdinary Heroes. She debuted on December 6 with the single "Happy Death Day". Then on January 26, 2022, JYP announced that they would debut on February 22 a new girl group called Nmixx with their debut single Ad Mare. On February 10, 2022, JYP expanded their strategic partnership with Republic Records after Twice began to flourish in the United States. Following the announcement, Republic signed Stray Kids and Itzy. In April, it was reported that JYPE's market capitalization had soared from 1.33 trillion from the previous year to 2.24 trillion won, increasing by 1 trillion won in the first three months of 2022. The Korean Stock Exchange reported that on April 8, prices the company's stock soared to 66,200 won, breaking its all-time high and marking the first time JYP Entertainment entered the 60,000 range since its listing in August 2001. days]. On July 12, all members of Twice renewed their contracts with JYP Entertainment. September 26, everyone members of Day6 also extended their contracts with the label. In the summer of 2023, JYP launched two new survival shows "A2K" (the first American group under a Korean agency) and "Nizi Project Season 2" (Japanese boy group).
